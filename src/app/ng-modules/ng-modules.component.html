An NgModule has two main responsibilities:

  • Declaring components, directives, and pipes that belong to the NgModule
  • Inject providers to the components, directives, and pipes that import the NgModule

Properties

Declarations: Declares the components, directives, and pipes that belong to the NgModule.

Imports: Habilitates the dependencies for the components, directives, and pipes declared in the NgModule.

Exports: An NgModule can export its declared components, directives, and pipes such that they're available to other components and NgModules

Providers: This property is used to inject services into the classes that belong to the NgModule.

forRoot and forChild pattern

The forRoot pattern is used to instanciate a service only once in the root of the application. This is useful when you want to share the same instance of a service across the entire application. It will be initialized at the bootstrap of the application

The forChild pattern is used to instanciate a service within a lazy-loaded module. Will be shared between the component and their children. It will be initialized at the render of the component
